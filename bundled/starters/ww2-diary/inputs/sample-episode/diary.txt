June 6. We woke before dawn to the low thud of guns beyond the harbor.
By noon, the rain had soaked every letter in my pack, but I kept Mother's photograph dry.
A medic traded me half a pencil for a tin cup of tea.
At dusk, the road filled with engines and voices, all of us pretending not to count the missing.
I write by candle stub tonight, grateful for one quiet hour and afraid to trust it.
