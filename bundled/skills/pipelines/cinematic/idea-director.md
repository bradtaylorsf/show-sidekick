# Idea Director - Cinematic Pipeline

## When To Use

Use this stage to turn the user's goal into a concrete cinematic promise that can be executed with generated motion, narration or dialogue, and a restrained visual grammar.

## Shared Visual Contract

Reference `bundled/skills/_shared/video-prompting.md` whenever you describe candidate shots, reference videos, or generated motion. Do not collapse cinematic intent into mood adjectives; describe what the viewer sees and how the camera moves.

## Process

1. Identify the audience, platform, target duration, subject, and intended viewer action.
2. State the cinematic promise as a testable outcome: what the piece makes the viewer understand, feel, or decide.
3. List known constraints: supplied references, characters, brand assets, must-use lines, prohibited imagery, and budget.
4. Flag any reference-driven requirements that must flow into the five-aspect structure later.
5. Select one primary idea and record rejected alternatives in the decision log.

## Quality Gate

- the brief is specific enough for three different proposal directions,
- motion is essential to the idea,
- reference requirements are captured without promising a carbon copy,
- the next stage has enough context to lock audio architecture.
